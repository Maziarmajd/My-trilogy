The Godfather Trilogy

The Godfather

Release Year: 1972

  • Director: Francis Ford Coppola
  • Starring:Marlon Brando as Don Vito Corleone, Al Pacino as Michael Corleone, James Caan as Santino "Sonny" Corleone, Robert Duvall as Tom Hagen, Diane Keaton as Kay Adams-Corleone
  • he Godfather follows the Corleone crime family, headed by Don Vito Corleone. The story revolves around the intricate world of organized crime, power struggles, family dynamics, and loyalty. Michael Corleone, initially reluctant to join the family business, is drawn into a world of violence and betrayal as he becomes his father's successor.

The The Godfather Part II

Release Year: 1974

  • Director: Francis Ford Coppola
  • Starring: Al Pacino as Michael Corleone, Robert Duvall as Tom Hagen, Diane Keaton as Kay Adams-Corleone, Robert De Niro as Young Vito Corleone, John Cazale as Fredo Corleone
  • The Godfather Part II tells a parallel story set in two different time periods. It explores the rise of a young Vito Corleone, as he establishes himself as a powerful mob boss. Simultaneously, it follows the struggles of Michael Corleone, who faces challenges in expanding and maintaining the family empire while dealing with personal betrayals and consequences of his actions.

The Godfather Part III

Release Year: 1990

  • Director: Francis Ford Coppola
  • Starring: Al Pacino as Michael Corleone, Diane Keaton as Kay Adams-Corleone, Andy Garcia as Vincent Mancini, Sofia Coppola as Mary Corleone, Talia Shire as Connie Corleone
  • The Godfather Part III takes place years after the events of the first two films. Michael Corleone, now older and seeking redemption, tries to legitimize his family business. However, he finds himself drawn back into the world of crime and power as he navigates the complexities of his personal and professional relationships, leading to tragic consequences.
